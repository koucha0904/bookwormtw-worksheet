  - q: Why is Christmas in summer in Australia?
    a: Australia is in the Southern Hemisphere, so its seasons are flipped. December is the middle of summer, which is why many Australian families have a Christmas BBQ at the beach.
  - q: Is a koala a bear?
    a: A koala is not a bear — it's a marsupial, related to kangaroos. The name "koala" comes from an Aboriginal word meaning "no drink", because koalas get most of their water from eucalyptus leaves.
  - q: Where is the Great Barrier Reef?
    a: It lies off the coast of Queensland in northeastern Australia. At around 2,300 km long, it's the largest coral reef system on Earth and one of the few natural features visible from space.
  - q: Why do kangaroos hop instead of running?
    a: Kangaroo legs work like springs — hopping uses their tendons elastically, so the faster they go, the less energy they use. It's one of nature's most efficient designs.

## What's inside this worksheet?

The 7-page Australia worksheet is built around "the extraordinary southern continent":

1. **North vs South hemispheres**: why is Christmas in summer here?
2. **Map of Australia**: six states, two territories — where is Canberra?
3. **Marvelous marsupials**: koalas, kangaroos, Tasmanian devils, possums.
4. **Great Barrier Reef ecology**: corals, clownfish, sea turtles, and the threats they face.
5. **Aboriginal culture**: Australia's First Peoples have lived here for over 60,000 years.
6. **Sydney Opera House**: a building shaped like sails — who designed it?
7. **Aussie English**: G'day, mate, barbie — try them out.
