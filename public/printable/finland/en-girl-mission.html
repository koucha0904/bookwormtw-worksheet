🌞🌑 Mission 1: Where Did the Sun Go?

Finland is far north, so summer days can stay bright very late and winter days can be quite short. This dramatic rhythm of light is one of Finland's most famous natural features.

✅ Activity A: True or False

In Finland, the sky can still be bright at 2 a.m. in summer. (True / False)
In winter, Helsinki has only about 6 hours of daylight each day. (True / False)
The true midnight sun happens only inside the Arctic Circle. (True / False)
Finnish people may need thick curtains to sleep in summer. (True / False)

Quick fact: the full midnight sun happens north of the Arctic Circle. In southern cities like Helsinki, summer nights stay very bright, but not always as full 24-hour daylight.
